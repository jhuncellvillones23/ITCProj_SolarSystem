    SUN

  • The Sun is the center of our Solar System and the primary source of energy for life on Earth.
    It is a massive, glowing ball of hot gases primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.

    Below are its key characteristics:

    • 1. SIZE AND STRUCTURE

  • DIAMETER: Approximately 1.39 million km (865,000 miles), about 109 times the diameter of Earth.

  • MASS: About 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg, roughly 333,000 times the mass of Earth.

  • VOLUME: Can hold about 1.3 million Earths.

  • COMPOSITION: Mostly hydrogen (74%) and helium (24%), with trace amounts of heavier elements.
