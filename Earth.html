    PLANET EARTH

  • Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often called Earth's twin because of its similar size and mass.
    However, its environment is vastly different. Below are the key characteristics of Venus:

    • 1. SIZE AND STRUCTURE

  • DIAMETER: Approximately 12,742 km (7,918 miles)

  • MASS: 5.97 × 10²⁴ kg, making it the largest terrestrial planet.

  • SHAPE: An oblate spheroid, slightly flattened at the poles and bulging at the equator.

  • SURFACE COMPOSITION: About 71% water and 29% land, including continents, islands, mountains, deserts, and other features.

  • CORE: A solid inner core and liquid outer core, primarily composed of iron and nickel, surrounded by a mantle and crust.

    • 2. ATMOSPHERE

  • COMPOSITION: Earth's atmosphere is made up of:
    - 78% nitrogen
    - 21% oxygen
    - 1% other gases     (argon, carbon dioxide, water vapor, etc.).

  • OZONE LAYER: Protects life by absorbing harmful ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the Sun.

  • WEATHER: Earth experiences dynamic weather systems due to its atmosphere and liquid water.
