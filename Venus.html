    PLANET VENUS

  • Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often called Earth's twin because of its similar size and mass.
    However, its environment is vastly different. Below are the key characteristics of Venus:

    • 1. SIZE AND STRUCTURE

  • DIAMETER: Approximately 12,104 km (7,521 miles), about 95% of Earth's diameter.

  • MASS: 4.87 × 10²⁴ kg, about 82% of Earth's mass.

  • SURFACE: Rocky and dominated by volcanic plains, mountains, and craters.

    • 2. ATMOSPHERE

  • COMPOSITION: Venus has a thick atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide (96.5%)
    with small amounts of nitrogen and trace gases like sulfur dioxide.

  • CLOUDS: Dense clouds of sulfuric acid completely cover the planet, making it reflective and bright.

  • PRESSURE: Surface pressure is about92 times that of Earth’s, equivalent to being 900 meters (3,000 feet) underwater.

  • GREENHOUSE EFFECT: The thick carbon dioxide atmosphere traps heat, causing arunaway greenhouse effect.

    • 3. TEMPERATURE EXTREMES

  • SURFACE TEMPERATURE: Venus is the hottest planet in the Solar System, with surface temperatures averaging 465°C (869°F), hot enough to melt lead.

  • The high temperature is constant across day and night, as well as poles and equator.
