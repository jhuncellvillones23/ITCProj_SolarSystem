    PLANET MERCURY

  • Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System, orbiting closest to the Sun.
    Here are its key characteristics:

    • 1. SIZE AND STRUCTURE

  • DIAMETER: About 4,880 km (3,032 miles) – roughly 38% the size of Earth.

  • MASS: 3.3 × 10²³ kg, making it the second least massive planet.

  • SURFACE: Rocky and heavily cratered, resembling Earth's Moon.

  • CORE: Mercury has a large iron core that accounts for about 75% of its radius, with a relatively thin silicate crust and mantle.

    • 2. ORBIT AND ROTATION

  • DISTANCE FROM THE SUN: About 57.9 million km (36 million miles), or 0.39 AU (Astronomical Units).

  • ROTATION PERIOD: Mercury rotates very slowly on its axis, taking 59 Earth days to complete one rotation (sidereal day).

  • DAY-NIGHT CYCLE: Due to its slow rotation and fast orbit, one solar day (sunrise to sunrise) lasts about 176 Earth days.

    • 3. TEMPERATURE EXTREMES

  • Mercury experiences the greatest temperature fluctuations in the Solar System:

  • DAYTIME: Can reach up to 430°C (800°F) due to its proximity to the Sun.

  • NIGHTTIME: Drops as low as -180°C (-290°F) because it has no atmosphere to retain heat.
